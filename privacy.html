Introduction

At Restore | Specialist Pelvic Physiotherapy I am committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information responsibly, securely and transparently.

This Privacy Policy explains how I collect, use, store, protect and share your personal information when you:

  • Book or attend physiotherapy appointments
  • Contact the practice
  • Complete registration forms, questionnaires or consent forms
  • Visit my website
  • Receive physiotherapy assessment, treatment or advice

As a pelvic health physiotherapist, I process personal information, including health information, in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018 and professional standards applicable to physiotherapy practice.

Lawful Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, personal information is processed under one or more of the following lawful bases:

Contract

Processing is necessary to provide physiotherapy services requested by you.

Legal Obligation

Processing is necessary to comply with legal, regulatory and professional obligations.

Legitimate Interests

Processing is necessary for the effective management and operation of the practice while respecting your rights and freedoms.

Consent

Processing may be based on your consent where required, such as certain marketing communications.

Healthcare Provision

Special category health information is processed because it is necessary for the provision of healthcare and treatment and is subject to professional confidentiality obligations.

Confidentiality

All information disclosed during consultations is treated as confidential.

Information will only be disclosed where:

  • You have given permission
  • It is necessary for your healthcare
  • There is a legal obligation to disclose information
  • There are safeguarding concerns requiring disclosure
  • Disclosure is required by a court, regulator or other lawful authority

Sharing Your Information

Where necessary and appropriate, information may be shared with:

  • Your GP

  • Professional advisers such as accountants, solicitors and insurers

Information is only shared when necessary and subject to appropriate confidentiality and security measures.

Data Security

I take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • Secure password-protected systems
  • Encrypted devices where appropriate
  • Secure cloud-based clinical record systems
  • Restricted access to personal information
  • Secure storage and disposal procedures
  • Regular review of security measures

While every reasonable effort is made to protect personal information, no electronic transmission or storage system can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

How Long Information Is Retained

Personal information is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected and to meet legal, professional and regulatory obligations.

Clinical records are generally retained in accordance with professional guidance and applicable legal requirements.

Retention periods may vary depending on:

  • Patient age
  • Nature of treatment
  • Legal requirements
  • Regulatory requirements
  • Potential legal claims

When records are no longer required, they will be securely destroyed or permanently deleted.

International Transfers

Where personal information is transferred outside the United Kingdom, appropriate safeguards will be implemented to ensure your information remains protected in accordance with UK GDPR requirements.
